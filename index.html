“Middle Eastern”

I like chai with my conversations,
not chai tea, only chai;
its strong and intoxicating fumes,
the lapping waves of...
home—
and there you are again: nostalgia

My mother would take a sip of chai,
and follow with it, that familiar groan,
never perfect—Dee's chai—yet my favorite,
and then we would sit on that impossibly small balcony,
ponder, sonder, and weave ebullient conversation

Mum would curse the crows that crowded the tamarind trees,
the trees we'd hurl stones at after school for a snack,
and as time went on, they'd hang up more targets for us,
their precious veiny hands and the shadows they'd cast upon us,
yes, the trees were also an adult to my childlike eyes

My people, from the East to the West and farther,
people of Deodars, Dates, Sycamores, Argans and Olives,
were born in the sap, to seep through the bark,
the mystery of exploitation and generous, generous land,
I call my home only where free, my people may stand,

so...
        I don't mind your questions—no, they aren't ridiculous,
To grow is to learn—from people within and beside you,
And yes for the hundredth time today,
I say I'm not Middle Eastern—but we're as one every other way,
because my people are your people and your people are mine too
